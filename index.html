Aircraft carriers play a crucial role in India’s strategic posture and regional stability. Their presence in the Indian Ocean region acts as a deterrent against potential adversaries. Additionally, aircraft carriers enhance India’s maritime capability by providing a self-generating supply of humanitarian assistance during disasters. They can swiftly respond to any crises, reinforcing peace and stability in the region.


Exhibits & Attractions

Perched atop a plateau overlooking the vast expanse of the Arabian Sea, the museum is divided into two distinct sections: an outdoor area with aircraft and engines on display, and an indoor section with various other exhibits. The outdoor section has the imposing Super Constellation, Sea Hawk, Sea Harrier, Alize, Islander, and Kamov 25, among others, to make a total of 15 aircraft on display. The indoor section has exhibits ranging from scale models of all current and decommissioned aircraft as well as all four aircraft carriers to have served in the Navy, milestones in the history of Aviation, Naval Aviation and Indian Naval Aviation, safety and survival equipment and information about all Naval Air Stations and Squadrons.

About Us

The Naval Aviation Museum, Asia’s first museum of its kind, stands along the Vasco-Bogmalo road, a mere six kilometers from the city of Vasco-da-Gama. Established in October 1998, this unique museum serves to preserve the rich history of Indian Naval Aviation.
